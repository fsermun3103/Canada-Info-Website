Toronto, Ontario

DIVERSITY, ATTRACTIONS & URBAN LIFE

Canada's largest and most multicultural city, Toronto combines iconic attractions like the CN Tower, world-class museums, lively neighbourhoods, and year-round festivals. It is a true cosmopolitan hub where cultures from every corner of the world meet, shaping a rich scene of food, art, sports, and entertainment. From vibrant districts like Kensington Market and Distillery District to waterfront walks along Lake Ontario, the city offers endless ways to explore. Its dynamic atmosphere blends international flavour with big-city energy, making Toronto a gateway to modern Canadian life.

Vancouver, British Columbia

BEACHES, HIKING TRAILS & SKI RESORTS

Vancouver is a stunning city where urban life blends seamlessly with nature. You can walk along oceanfront paths in the morning, explore lush forests in the afternoon, all without leaving the city. From the panoramic views in Stanley Park to the thrill of crossing the Capilano Suspension Bridge. Surrounded by mountains and the Pacific Ocean, Vancouver offers endless opportunities for hiking, cycling, kayaking, and skiing just minutes from downtown. Its relaxed atmosphere, and constant connection to the outdoors make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both excitement and tranquility in one unforgettable place.

Montréal, Québec

EUROPEAN CHARM, FESTIVALS & CULINARY CAPITAL

Montréal blends old-world charm with vibrant urban energy. Wander through Old Montréal's historic streets, admire the stunning Notre-Dame Basilica. The city comes alive with music, art, and festivals throughout the year, filling its streets with color and rhythm, while its neighborhoods buzz with creativity, street art, and local flavor. From lively markets to elegant squares and scenic viewpoints like Mount Royal, every area invites exploration. With its unique mix of history, gastronomy, and culture, Montréal offers an unforgettable experience unlike anywhere else in North America, where European elegance meets modern Canadian spirit.
